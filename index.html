Research Areas

Research Interest

Rating Prediction with Review Network Feedback

At the initial stage, when recommender systems were proposed, the researchers focused only on users' rating activities. But only users' posted ratings are insufficient for accurate rating prediction of an unknown item. So now the researchers are focusing on explicit feedback/response and implicit feedback/response as users' side information to predict rating accurately. We have proposed a score that indicates users' characteristics in form of users' reliability and influential power in her network. This score is evaluated based on the topological structure of user-item bipartite network. We named this score Review Network Feedback. We evaluate predicted rating values based on this score.

Heterogeneous Trust Relation-based Recommender Systems

The emergence and advancement of recommendation systems use in recommending suitable items to the right online users based on their preferences. Users can share their feedback about their purchasing items through social media. The social trust-related information among users is vital because we share opinions with our neighbors or are adapted to take feedback from our reliable online neighborhoods. This social trust relation is a crucial feature in recommendation systems to overcome the problem of data sparsity

Computational Model for Recommender System

Although users' side information and the selection of features have a crucial role in an efficient recommendation, model architecture is also a vital part of an efficient recommendation system for learning users' side information. We are focusing on generalized model, hybrid model and Graph Neural Network architecture.

Indicator of Product Sale

The conventional way of analyzing the quality and sentiment of reviews and ratings, volume of sales or product prices is not enough for establishing a strong regression between these parameters and future product sales. Most of the existing works ignore the heterogeneous positional and influential effects of individual users' reviews and ratings. Most of the existing works ignore the heterogeneous positional and influential effects of individual users reviews and ratings. For the realization of these effects, we use a review network i.e., a bipartite network between users and items based on the users' reviewing activities. In this chapter, we present a concept named Network Promoter Score (NePS) based on the reliability and positional influence of each user in the network. In-depth experiments on online review datasets show that NePS emerges as a strong indicator of product sales and can be remarkably futuristic compared to the existing parameters. Furthermore, we propose a predictive modeling technique to estimate the product sales of a company based on NePS.

Rating-based Recommender System

Most of the existing recommender systems understand the preference level of users based on user-item interaction ratings. If there is a poor reflection from a user on an item, existing recommender systems assume that these items are not in the user's preferred categories. The poor reflection of a user to a specific item cannot be treated as a wrong recommendation immediately and the good reflection of a user to a specific item can be treated as an accurate recommendation. A user purchases items based on her preferable zones, but her rating behavior activity relates to her characteristics. We have to recognize whether a user is a positive, a negative, and/or a reliable reviewer. When the existing approaches learn the latent feature vector of a user from similar behaviors among the users' rating activities or preference degree, they overlook the characteristics of the users in most of the studies. Our target is to investigate whether the item is not in the user’s preferred category, whether the user is dissatisfied with the quality of a favorite item or whether the user gives ratings randomly/casually. Based on this investigation and users' preferences we evaluate the user-item interaction score. Then based on this user-item interaction score, we evaluate each user's top-ranking preferable items.
